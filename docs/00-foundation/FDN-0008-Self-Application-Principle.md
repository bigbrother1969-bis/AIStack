# FDN-0008 — AIStack Self-Application Principle

## Principle

AIStack shall govern its own knowledge heritage before governing the heritage of any other information system.

The project itself is the first validation environment of the Governed Heritage Engineering methodology.

Every architectural principle introduced in AIStack should first be demonstrated on AIStack itself.

## Rationale

Architecture should be validated through practice rather than intention.

Applying the methodology to AIStack itself ensures that:

- architectural decisions remain practical;
- governance mechanisms are exercised continuously;
- documentation evolves with implementation;
- transmission mechanisms are validated under real conditions;
- the project continuously demonstrates its own value.

## Consequences

AIStack is simultaneously:

- the product;
- the reference implementation;
- the first governed heritage;
- the validation platform;
- the primary case study of Governed Heritage Engineering.

## Architectural Consequences

Every major architectural capability should first be implemented on AIStack itself.

Examples include:

- Knowledge Providers;
- Knowledge Policies;
- Knowledge Graph;
- Context Bundles;
- Knowledge Catalog;
- Publication Pipelines;
- Governance Reports.

Only after successful validation should these capabilities be proposed for governing external information systems.

## Relationship with the Foundation

This principle complements:

- FDN-0004 — Governed Heritage;
- FDN-0007 — Governed Engineering Cycle.

It transforms AIStack from a software project into the first operational demonstration of the Knowledge Operating System.
