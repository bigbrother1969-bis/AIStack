# STD-0101 — Writing Rules

## Purpose

This standard defines the official writing rules for every canonical Knowledge Artifact.

Its objective is to maximize readability, consistency and long-term maintainability.

---

# Language

Canonical Knowledge Artifacts shall be written in English.

Exceptions may exist for books, presentations or user-facing communication.

---

# Writing Style

Documentation shall be:

- concise;
- precise;
- explicit;
- factual;
- timeless whenever possible.

Avoid unnecessary prose.

Avoid marketing language.

Avoid implementation-specific wording unless required.

---

# Terminology

Official terminology shall come from the Foundation Glossary.

A concept shall always be designated by the same official name.

Synonyms should be avoided.

---

# Sections

Each section shall answer a single question.

Prefer several short sections over long narrative text.

---

# Lists

Lists shall be preferred whenever they improve readability.

Items should follow a consistent grammatical structure.

---

# Diagrams

Prefer Mermaid diagrams whenever they improve understanding.

Diagrams are Knowledge Artifacts.

They should remain simple and maintainable.

---

# Examples

Examples shall illustrate the rule.

Examples shall never redefine the rule.

---

# Normative Vocabulary

The following words have normative meaning.

- SHALL
- SHALL NOT
- SHOULD
- SHOULD NOT
- MAY

Their usage follows RFC 2119 conventions.

---

# References

Whenever possible, reference canonical Knowledge Artifacts by identifier.

Example:

- FDN-0005
- STD-0100
- ADR-0001
- CMP-0001
